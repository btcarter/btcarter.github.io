Installing Neuroimaging Software

You are going to need a few software packages in order analyze your imaging data. Luckily everything we are going to use is free so you won't be out any money. The bad news is that this is probably going to take quite a bit of time (an hour or two). So sit down and get settled.

Analysis of Functional NeuroImages (AFNI)

Go to the Installation and Background Page on the AFNI website. Follow the installation guide instructions for your flavor of Linux, Apple or Windows operating system. You can omit the section of the instructions titled Preparing for Bootcamp (it's just material used during the NIMH's courses, so if you do install it that's not a problem). AFNI is free software written and maintained by Bob Cox and his team at the NIH. It can be used to analyze a number of different types of neuroimaging data. We are going to use it for our functional data.
